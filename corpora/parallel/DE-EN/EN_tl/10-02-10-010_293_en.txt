Even before the earthquake, Haiti was a poor country without guaranteed local food supplies for more than two million people and with hundreds of thousands of orphans on the streets or in homes.
For the people of Haiti it is, of course, important that the infrastructure and the institutions of the state are rebuilt in the long term.
However, we must not forget that the distribution of aid is not going smoothly and that many women and children are in a very vulnerable situation.
We must act cautiously in this case.
The motion for a resolution appears to do justice to the majority of problems and for this reason, I have voted in favour of it.
