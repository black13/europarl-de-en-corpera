Mr President, Mr President of the Commission, the motion for a resolution tabled by the Committee on Institutional Affairs is quite right to complain about the status quo tendency of the Intergovernmental Conference's work so far.
But has this Parliament done enough to overcome this policy of timidity and lack of imagination?
Yet the task that lies before is plain for all to see.
That task is to transform an economic community, an area without internal borders, into a union of nations, a political union, like it says in the Maastricht Treaty.
There is no democratic way of creating such a union other than by entering into a process that will produce a constitution, in order to overcome Europe's East-West divide, thereby providing an initial response to the enormous challenges facing society today.
